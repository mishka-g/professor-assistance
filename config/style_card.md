# Style Card

## Voice & tone
- **Formal and impersonal**: The author uses a formal, third-person voice throughout. Phrases like "We report" are used to introduce findings but maintain an objective tone.
  - Example: "We report vertically-illuminated, resonant cavity enhanced, graphene-Si Schottky photodetectors (PDs) operating at 1550nm."

## Sentence & paragraph structure
- **Complex sentences**: Sentences are typically long and complex, often containing multiple clauses to convey detailed information.
  - Example: "In free-space optical communications (FSO)[2–4] and light-radars (LIDARs)[5, 6], this minimizes the propagation losses in fog[3] and humid conditions[4], due to lower optical absorption and scattering compared to wavelengths< 1µm."
- **Paragraph openings**: Paragraphs often begin with a broad statement followed by detailed elaboration.
  - Example: "Near infrared (NIR) photodetection at 1550nm is of paramount importance for a variety of applications, ranging from optical communications[1–4] to remote sensing[5, 6]."

## Preferred terminology
- **Technical terms**: The author consistently uses precise technical terms and abbreviations.
  - Example: "Schottky PDs", "internal photoemission (IPE)", "near infrared (NIR)".
- **Specific spellings/casing**: Terms are often capitalized when they refer to specific concepts or technologies.
  - Examples: "FSO" for Free-Space Optical Communications, "LIDAR" for Light Detection and Ranging.

## Terms / phrasings to avoid
- **Generic or informal terms**: Avoid using generic or overly casual language that lacks precision.
  - Example: Phrases like "these devices work well" should be replaced with more specific technical descriptions such as "our PDs exhibit high responsivity."

## Formatting conventions
- **Units and abbreviations**: Units are consistently abbreviated in a standard scientific format (e.g., mA/W, nm).
  - Example: "Our devices have wavelength-dependent photoresponse with external (internal) responsivity ∼20mA/W (0.25A/W)."
- **Citation phrasing**: Citations are typically placed within square brackets and immediately follow the referenced material.
  - Example: "... due to lower optical absorption and scattering compared to wavelengths< 1µm, [3]."
- **Number formatting**: Numbers are generally written as numerals unless they begin a sentence or refer to specific measurements (e.g., "1550nm").
  - Example: "The spectral-selectivity may be further tuned by varying the cavity resonant wavelength."

This style card captures the meticulous and formal tone of the author's writing, emphasizing precision in terminology and detailed explanations.
