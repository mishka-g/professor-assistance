# Style Card

## Voice & tone
- **Formal and impersonal**: The author uses a formal, objective tone throughout. Phrases like "We report" are used to introduce findings but generally avoids personal pronouns beyond that.
- **Use of "we"**: Used sparingly for collaborative work statements ("We report").
- **Degree of hedging**: Minimal hedging; the text is direct and confident in its assertions.

## Sentence & paragraph structure
- **Sentence length**: Typically medium to long, with an average sentence length around 20-30 words. Example: "Our devices have wavelength-dependent photoresponse with external (internal) responsivity∼20mA/W (0.25A/W)."
- **Connective phrasing**: Uses a variety of connectives such as "to obtain," "resulting in," and "due to." Example: "To obtain spectral selectivity and enhance responsivity, the PDs are integrated with an optical cavity."
- **Paragraph openings**: Often begins paragraphs with topic sentences that introduce key concepts. Example: "Near infrared (NIR) photodetection at 1550nm is of paramount importance for a variety of applications."

## Preferred terminology
- **Terms consistently used**:
    - **"photodetectors" or "PDs"**: Refers to devices as PDs frequently.
    - **"internal photoemission (IPE)"**: Used in the context of Schottky junctions.
    - **"resonant cavity enhanced"**: Describes the enhancement mechanism used in photodetectors.
    - **"Schottky junction"**: Key term for describing device structure.

## Terms / phrasings to avoid
- **Generic or informal terms**:
    - "Many": Avoids using vague quantifiers like "many."
    - Informal phrases: Phrases such as "a lot of," "pretty good," etc., are not used.
    
## Formatting conventions
- **Units**: Uses standard SI units, e.g., mA/W for responsivity. Example: "external (internal) responsivity∼20mA/W (0.25A/W)."
- **Abbreviations**: Commonly uses abbreviations like PDs, FSO, LIDARs, OCT.
    - Full form provided initially and used consistently thereafter.
- **Citation phrasing**: Uses square brackets for inline citations, e.g., [1–4].
- **Number formatting**:
    - Numbers are generally written in numerals unless starting a sentence. Example: "1550nm."
    - Temperature is formatted with degree symbol (e.g., 650°C).

This style card captures the meticulous and formal tone of the author's writing, focusing on clarity and precision in terminology and formatting conventions.
